१. आयुष्कामीयम्

Verse 1

रागादिरोगान् सततानुषक्तानशेषकायप्रसृतानशेषान् ।
औत्सुक्यमोहारतिदान् जघान योऽपूर्ववैद्याय नमोऽस्तु तस्मै ॥१॥
rāgādirogān satatānuṣaktānaśeṣakāya-prasṛtānaśeṣān । autsukya-mohāratidān jaghāna yo’pūrvavaidyāya namo’stu tasmai ॥
छन्दः (Metre)

वृत्तम्: उपजातिः

Metre: Upajāti

लक्षणम् (Definition)

संस्कृतम् :
अनन्तरोदीरितलक्ष्मभाजौ पादौ यदीयावुपजातयस्ताः ।
इत्थं किलान्यास्वपि मिश्रितासु वदन्ति जातिष्विदमेव नाम ॥

Transliteration :
anantarodīrita-lakṣma-bhājau pādau yadīyāv upajātayas tāḥ | itthaṃ kilānyāsv api miśritāsu vadanti jātiṣv idam eva nāma ||

Meaning :
A metre is called Upajāti when its quarters (pādas) are composed by combining the two closely related metres Indravajrā and Upendravajrā in any arrangement. Because it arises from the mixture of these two metres, it is known as Upajāti (“a derived or mixed class”).

अर्थः (Meaning)

Salutations to that unparalleled physician who destroyed the afflictions beginning with attachment, which remain constantly associated with living beings, pervade the entirety of embodied existence, and generate anxiety, delusion, and dissatisfaction.
